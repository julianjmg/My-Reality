My Reality

Downtown, the buildings stretch up into the sky looking down on the people commuting to work. The air is filled with a brisk hustle and the people move in a semi orderly fashion, they’ve been training all their lives to do this. Aaron slides in with the people heading to work struggling to match their pace. Off in the distance, he can hear the clicking of a megaphone and the voice of someone about to speak. Despite his legs being fatigued he pushes them even harder into the concrete and increases his pace. Even with his efforts of creating distance, the words of the Diviner’s were destined to meet his ears. “Whichever direction you go, whether to or away from me, you will reach me. It is as fate has it written,” The Diviner says. No matter where he goes or where he attempts to hide, the Diviner’s voice remains a constant volume no matter the distance. Even if he had ear plugs, His words would still ring true. Aaron looks around and the people are unfazed by the sounds. They continue walking as programmed. Aaron continues moving away from the sound, but at a slower pace. Moving his attention slightly from the Diviner, he is able to hear the conversations of the people going to work, the blaring ringtones, yelling, complaining, sadness, anger. He grounds his feet and listens for the words to find him. “I see you are willing to listen. I will come to you,” says the Diviner. The pedestrians continue to drone on as Aaron stands still on the sidewalk. A large man in a rush bumps into him causing to lose balance and fall down. From the people crossing A woman in business casual attire exits her morning trance after seeing Aaron knocked down to the ground and attempts to help him.

Julian Giles
