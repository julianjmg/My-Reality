My Reality

By Julian Giles

They finally reach their destination. At the end of the alley, there is a lone doorway that looks completely out of place from the gray buildings they’ve been walking between. The door is wooden with a yellow door frame. The air and buildings around it wave like a mirage. It warps and twists from this point. It is the origin of the chaos. The Diviner walks to the door and moves through it without pushing the door open. As he walks through the door stretches and rebounds back into place. Aaron wipes the sweat from his palms and proceeds through the door. As he walks through, he feels an immense amount of pressure and static running around his body. When he appears in the room. It's bright and the walls are all white. The Diviner stands against the wall camouflaged, only the gold embroidered eye contrasts from it. Beside the Diviner lies a white cuboidal table and resting upon it is an old yellowed scroll and a fruit . As Aaron approaches the table, the corners of the room begin to stretch and it feels like the room is spinning. It's that same feeling of vertigo when looking down from the apartment rooftop. The edge is dangerous, but there is still this draw that pulls him along like strings of fate twisting together to create a thread. The Diviner, no longer visible says, “You must read aloud and eat from the fruit. You will then have all of your answers.” The Diviner’s voice travels throughout the room with no walls getting lost in space and time as the sound travels. Aaron’s hands are magnets pulling the paper and fruit toward him. An incantation is written on the page and the words flow out from his mouth.
