My Reality

By Julian Giles

A flock of birds fly away as Aaron approaches a park. His hands are clammy and his thoughts are muddled with delusions of the Diviner. He searches the patterns of peoples clothing, logos of businesses, and building murals for the Diviner’s sigil. It is a sickness that his mind can’t help but indulge in. He briefly rests at the ledge of a park fountain. He looks into the water and sees his wavering reflection. The beard that he once shaved twice a day has grown out into a face that is unrecognizable to him. His eyebags tug on his face keeping his eyes open. The water fountain is worn down and cracked on the side. Water pours out from it like a fresh wound, yet somehow it never runs out of water and it keeps on juggling the water, performing for the park’s people. He reaches into the fountain and splashes himself with water. The water runs off of his face down to his prickly chin and returns back to the fountain creating a small ripple. Aaron looks back to his reflection. Over his shoulder he sees the eye he’s been searching for. The diviner looms over his shoulder, giggling before he speaks, “This system failed you,” the words pressed into Aaron like icicles. “Run from me all you like, but the future is inevitable. You will know the true nature of this world. There is not much for you to lose now.” Aaron hastily turns around, but once again the Diviner is no longer there. He pushes himself off from the fountain and heads deeper into the city.
